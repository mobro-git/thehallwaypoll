Welcome to our first poll including results from the RTP campus! Previously, our poll was collecting data from but ONE geographic region. Thankfully, we have been able to expand our reach to the EPA RTP campus, both diversifying our pool of respondents and upping our N. We can now include an exploration of geographic differences in our polling results where we find points of interest.

Does HQ agree with RTP?

Now for the fun part: with two offices voting, we can finally ask whether HQ and RTP actually see things the same way, instead of just assuming it. For each question we run a two-proportion test comparing the two offices’ support for the overall winning option.

question Leader (overall) HQ share RTP share Gap (pts) p-value
Animal Scoping Snorkling 71% (27/38) 66% (23/35) 5% 0.812
Enjoying Nature Hike 82% (32/39) 82% (27/33) 0% 1.000
Swimming Ocean 57% (25/44) 64% (21/33) 7% 0.712
Volunteering Trash Pickup 45% (17/38) 59% (20/34) 14% 0.338

None of the four gaps clear statistical significance (all p > 0.2) — with samples this size we shouldn’t expect them to. But two results stand out:

  • Enjoying Nature is where the offices agree almost perfectly: HQ went 82% for Hiking and RTP went 82% — a gap of well under a point.
  • Volunteering is the sneaky one. The combined total above looked like a coin flip, but that’s actually two offices confidently disagreeing and canceling each other out: HQ leaned toward Invasive Species Removal while RTP leaned toward Trash Pickup, the widest office gap of the day. A “toss-up” in the combined numbers can quietly be hiding a real split underneath.

Since this is the first week with two offices, let’s define a running HQ–RTP Alignment Index: the correlation between HQ’s and RTP’s vote shares across every option this week. Right now it’s 0.9 — pretty well aligned, Volunteering’s split aside. We’ll track this number as more polls come in from both offices.
